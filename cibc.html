Connecting with Clients Through Gamification
Given our ever-changing world, communicating with our clients has become more important than ever. As such, CIBC needs to reflect these changes in the way we communicate. For the SLA summer project, my team researched variables that may impact communication (technology, preferences, etc.) before creating a survey to identify which generations would be impacted most. Utilizing both the primary data from our survey and tertiary data from outside sources, our group has identified the student segment (Gen Z) in particular as the segment with a largely evolving communications need. Our data shows that Gen Z would like to be engaged primarily through email; a major pain point (and opportunity) for Gen Z is the long wait times they face while banking. Our research indicates that Gen Z builds relationships with brands through a company’s core values, thus, our communications should reflect what CIBC stands for and believes in. Through building brand awareness and promoting core values, CIBC can improve its communication, brand perception, and client experience to capture the loyalty and business of Gen Z clients.
Project Date
2020
Challenge
The world is changing and how we connect and communicate - with and to - our clients, community, and employees, needs to change too. In this new world, what role should communications play in how we build strong relationships with our clients and employees?
Objective
  • Engage & attract Gen Z through Gamification and Incentives Program
  • Differentiate our Mobile Banking App from our competitors
  • Develop a Proto-Persona
  • Provide an innovative and unique take on financial literacy
  • Engage employees as clients
Findings
The questionnaire was completed by 17 users in post-secondary education. Firstly, most people enrol in courses to improve skills, learn at their own pace, and for career advancement. Next, most users are able to learn and retain the material well, and it takes them less time than anticipated to complete a course. Most users finish most of the courses they enrol in but rarely all or none at all, which shows that lack of motivation to complete courses is definitely prevalent in users when doing self-paced courses.
Solution
Our solution delivers personalization to users by integrating a social networking and gamification component on the LMS. Self-tests show instant feedback after completion. Virtual badges will be earned for completing courses and signing into the LMS consistently to make the user feel accomplished. The user can engage with other users through discussion posts. This facilitates social learning and emphasizes course content’s application. To address the cognitive limitations of attention and memory, the LMS shows users what concepts they are and are not yet proficient in. We will utilize the responding interface type, push notifications, to remind users about their courses.
